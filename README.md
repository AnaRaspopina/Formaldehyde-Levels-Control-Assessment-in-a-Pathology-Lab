# Formaldehyde Levels Monitoring in the Grossing Area of Anatomic Pathology lab Assessement and Conclusions

Formaldehyde levels control is an important Health&Safety matter in Anatomic Pathology Laboratories, as formaldehyde is a proven cancerogen and local irritant. 
The current acceptable formaldehyde level as required by WorkHealth New Zealand is 0.3 ppm (parts per million molecules). The concentration of formaldehyde must never exceed this limit in the working areas where workers do not wear protective gear. 
In this project I analysed the records collected from the portable measuring devices (PPM HTV-M Formaldehyde Meters) to answer the following questions:

- How stable are the formaldehyde levels in the working area
- What affects the concentration
- How reliable the devices are
- Should anything be changed in the area to improve the physical working conditions
- Can more people work in the area

The data was obtained from the downloaded records of the meter recording the ambient concentration of formaldehyde in the Grossing Area. This records are in CSV files and include formaldehyde concentration in ppm and mg per cubic meter, air temperature and humiduty.
